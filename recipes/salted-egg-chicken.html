Taiwanese Salted Egg Chicken

Taiwanese Salted Egg Chicken is a delicious and unique dish that combines the classic Taiwanese-style popcorn fried chicken with the popular Singaporean flavor of salted egg yolk. The dish features bite-sized pieces of crispy and juicy chicken that are coated in a rich and savory salted egg yolk sauce. To make Taiwanese Salted Egg Chicken, the chicken is first marinated with a blend of soy sauce, garlic, and other aromatic spices. It is then coated in a batter made with a mixture of flour and cornstarch, and deep-fried until golden brown and crispy. The crispy chicken pieces are then tossed in a decadent sauce made with salted egg yolk, butter, curry leaves, and other flavorful ingredients. The result is a deliciously savory and slightly sweet dish that is perfect as an appetizer or main course. Overall, Taiwanese Salted Egg Chicken is a creative and flavorful dish that combines the best of Taiwanese and Singaporean cuisine. It is a perfect example of how different culinary traditions can come together to create something truly unique and delicious.

Ingredients

for 6 servings

  • 2 lbs. boneless chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tbsp. soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp. garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp. Chinese five-spice powder
  • 1/4 tsp. black pepper
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch
  • 1 tsp. salt
  • 1/4 tsp. black pepper
  • Vegetable oil, for frying
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 4 salted egg yolks, mashed
  • 1/4 cup evaporated milk
  • 1 tbsp. curry leaves
  • 1 tsp. sugar
  • 1/4 tsp. salt
  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, garlic powder, Chinese five-spice powder, and black pepper.
  2. Add the chicken pieces to the marinade and toss until the chicken is evenly coated. Let the chicken marinate for at least 30 minutes, or up to overnight in the refrigerator.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, cornstarch, salt, and black pepper.
  4. Dredge the chicken pieces in the flour mixture, shaking off any excess.
  5. Preheat the vegetable oil in a deep-fryer or large pot to 375Â°F.
  6. Fry the chicken in batches until golden brown and crispy, about 5-7 minutes per batch. Drain the fried chicken on a paper towel-lined plate.
  7. In a separate pan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the mashed salted egg yolks, evaporated milk, curry leaves, sugar, and salt. Cook, stirring constantly, until the sauce is thick and creamy, about 3-4 minutes.
  8. Toss the fried chicken in the salted egg yolk sauce until the chicken pieces are coated evenly.
  9. Serve the Taiwanese Salted Egg Chicken hot, garnished with additional curry leaves, if desired.
